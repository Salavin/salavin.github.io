 I am Sam Lavin: music geek, audiophile, and avid technology enthusiast. Currently, I am the Production Intern at 88.5 KURE Ames Alternative, and also the Secretary and Accompanist for the Alpha Delta chapter of the Phi Mu Alpha Sinfonia Men's Music Fraternity. I am a Sophomore Computer Engineering student at Iowa State University pursuing minors in Music and Music Technology. When I graduate, I would like to work in Music Production and Audio Engineering.

A (semi) short auto-biography:

 All my life, I have always loved technology. I always loved messing with the Windows PC we had in our home (hence why I am still a Windows user to this day, despite its flaws) and finding all the in's and out's of it. I remember my first coding experience was around 6th or 7th grade when I discovered batch files. I found out that you could write programs with it using many many GO-TO statements, and so I began writing my own weird text-based Pokemon game. I believe I actually got decently far with writing it, especially for how little I knew of programming back then. Eventually, there became too many GO-TO statements, and I never saw the program again.

 From there, I continued to learn more about the world of programming and technology. I learned about Scratch: a visual, block-based programming language built out of MIT. I programmed a very large game which involved sending a rocket to space, fighting aliens, and then landing on the moon. My plan was that once you landed on the moon, you would have to fight more aliens, but the computer I was using at the time to program the game couldn't handle everything I was writing, and it became painfully slow, and I eventually had to move on. You can still find the game here!

 At this point, high school came along, and I got much more involved with extracurriculars and academics that didn't leave much time for experimenting with programming. This is when I started to become heavily involved with music. I was still involved with track, cross-country, show choir, and choir from middle-school, however they were obviously much more intense at the high-school level. I joined the marching band on first trumpet freshman year, however I encounter major problems with playing after having my braces taken out. After marching band, I moved to playing baritone for concert band, which was quite a change moving from a melodic-based treble clef instrument that is played in the key of Bb, to a bass clef instrument that is played in the key of C. However, playing in a different clef wasn't so much of a big deal thanks to the fact that I had been playing piano for 7 years at this point, and overall it ended up working out okay. I played baritone for marching band my sophomore year, and then was convinced to switch to tuba by one of my good tuba friends who was graduating. This was not as hard of a transition from baritone, and I ended up liking the tuba so much that I stuck with it to this day. Junior and senior years I auditioned for the Iowa All-State band on tuba, and was semi-accepted as first-alternate my senior year. I also picked up playing the bass guitar towards the end of my sophomore year, and joined the jazz band on bass for my junior and senior years.

 Needless to say, I became very musically-adept through high school. I earned many division I ratings at solo/ensemble contests, lettered almost every year in every ensemble I was in, and was recognized with the highest award in our band, the John Philip Sousa Award. My senior year, I went back and forth a lot, trying to decide if I wanted to do engineering or music in the future. Eventually, I decided engineering was the way to go. My freshman year of college, I loved computer engineering. However, that love did not continue into sophomore year.

 When I came to ISU, you could probably imagine that I became as much or more musically involved than I did in high school, and you would be right. I joined marching band on sousaphone, men's choir,a mixed choir, the Phi Mu Alpha Men's Music Fraternity, pep band on bass guitar, Cyclone Sound (a WGIWinds group), accompanied 2 musicals, and even played with the tuba studio for a while. Sometime sophomore year, I realized that my true passion was really with music, and I needed to change my career path to reflect that. At the time, I was working as a paid full-stack web developer for Iowa State IT Services.I ended up quitting this job in favor of becoming an unpaid production intern for 88.5 KURE AmesAlternative. While I still haven't had a lot of time there yet, I can at least tell I am on the right track for what I want to do. I have had many opportunities open up recently, and hopefully they show up somewhere on this website.

 Thank you for taking the time to read about me. Please feel free to take a look around the rest of the website!
